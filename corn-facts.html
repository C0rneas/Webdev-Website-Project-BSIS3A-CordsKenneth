Did You Know?

  • Corn is grown on every continent except Antarctica.
  • There are over 3,500 uses for corn products!
  • Popcorn is a type of corn, and it's the only type that pops.
  • The average ear of corn has about 800 kernels, arranged in 16 rows.
  • Corn is a key ingredient in more than 4,000 everyday products, from gasoline to toothpaste.
  • Corn was first domesticated over 10,000 years ago in southern Mexico.
  • There are over 5,000 different varieties of corn grown worldwide.
  • Corn is used to make biofuel, a renewable source of energy.
  • Corn is often used in the production of high-fructose corn syrup, a common sweetener.
  • The largest corn-producing country is the United States, followed by China and Brazil.
  • Corn is a member of the grass family, not a vegetable.
  • The stalk of corn can grow as tall as 10 feet or more, depending on the variety and growing conditions.
  • Corn plants can produce up to two ears of corn per stalk.
  • The husk of corn is sometimes used in making biodegradable packaging materials.
  • Corn is considered a grain, not a vegetable, even though it is consumed like one.
  • Indigenous people in the Americas have been cultivating corn for thousands of years.
  • Corn is used in the production of cornstarch, which is commonly used as a thickening agent in cooking.
  • Sweet corn is typically harvested while the kernels are still soft and sweet, unlike field corn which is dried for use in products like cornmeal.
  • Corn plants are pollinated by the wind, making them dependent on strong winds for reproduction.
  • Some varieties of corn, like blue corn, are naturally high in antioxidants and are used to make tortillas and chips.
  • Corn syrup, made from corn starch, is a common sweetener used in soft drinks, candies, and other processed foods.
  • Popcorn kernels can reach temperatures of up to 180°C (356°F) when they pop!
  • Corn has been genetically modified for various reasons, including increased resistance to pests and improved drought tolerance.
  • Corn was once considered a sacred plant by many Native American tribes, and it was often grown alongside beans and squash in a planting system known as the "Three Sisters."
  • The first commercially successful popcorn machine was invented in 1885 by Charles Cretors in Chicago.
